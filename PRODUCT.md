# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary: BD/licensing directors at mid-size pharma companies. They scan for in-licensing opportunities, need to surface high-fit assets fast, and report up to VP BD. Time-poor, pattern-matching experts who recognise signal quickly and distrust anything that feels like marketing. Secondary: TTO managers reviewing portfolio visibility, biotech VC analysts building deal flow, and EdenMarket participants (sellers listing assets, buyers submitting EOIs).

## Product Purpose

AI-powered biotech asset matchmaking. EdenRadar ingests signals from 400+ TTO scrapers and 32 scientific/patent/grant/pre-print sources, normalises them through a multi-stage pipeline, and generates buyer-facing intelligence: Scout search, ranked asset results, dossiers, match reports, Pipeline management, and a confidential deal marketplace (EdenMarket). Success means a BD director opens Scout and finds three genuinely relevant, accurately described assets they didn't know existed — and trusts the data enough to act on it.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious, fast, insider. The product conveys insider access and speed — like having a quant fund's deal-sourcing advantage applied to biotech licensing. Not a tool you browse; a tool that already knows what you're looking for.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Vercel-era AI SaaS cream**: off-white background, Inter font, subtle purple or gradient hero, "delightful" rounded cards — the default LLM wrapper aesthetic. EdenRadar should feel purpose-built for a specific domain, not assembled from a UI kit.
- **Pharma corporate**: clinical white + blue, stock photos of scientists, formal and cold. The product is for the people licensing from pharma, not pharma's brand team.

## Design Principles

1. **Intelligence without noise** — the platform surfaces signal and suppresses everything else. Information earns its place; decoration doesn't.
2. **Speed as respect** — BD directors move fast. Every interaction should feel immediate: instant feedback, no waiting-for-permission UX, tight information density.
3. **Insider confidence** — the product should feel like it knows things others don't. Data specificity, not reassurance copy, is what builds trust.
4. **Multi-portal coherence** — five portal identities (Scout/emerald, Lab/violet, Discovery/amber, Market/indigo) share a system but feel distinct. Portal colour is a navigational signal, not decoration.
5. **Precision over polish** — a precisely labelled metric beats a beautifully styled empty state. Correctness and legibility come before visual flourish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced-motion already handled via `prefers-reduced-motion` in global CSS. No additional known user accommodations beyond standard contrast and keyboard navigation.
