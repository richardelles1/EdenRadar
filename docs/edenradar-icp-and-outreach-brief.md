# EdenRadar — ICP & Outreach Brief (US Biotech, First Leads)

**Companion to:** `attached_assets/edenradar_first_leads.csv` (and `.xlsx`)
**List size:** 105 prospects · **Segments:** 44 Emerging · 31 Mid-cap · 15 Big Pharma · 15 VC/Studio
**Generated:** 2026-05-02 · **Re-run cadence:** every 90 days (B2B data decays ~30%/yr)

---

## 1. Why this list exists

EdenRadar surfaces licensable assets from US university tech-transfer offices, papers, patents, and trials. The buyers are the people who *consume* that supply: BD, Search & Evaluation, External Innovation, and the CSO/R&D leaders who sign the deals. Cold start, no warm intros, no installed base — so the list is built around the strongest single signal in B2B biotech: **somebody just got money or just got hired with a mandate to do exactly this**.

Each row is meant to become the first line of a cold email — the "Why Now" column is the opener.

## 4. Top 5 trigger types we hunted

Ranked by conversion strength on cold outreach in biotech BD:

1. **Series B/C/D close in last 90 days** — budget just unlocked, BD usually mandated to spend within 6 months. Source: company press release, Endpoints News, BioPharma Dive, Crunchbase.
2. **New CSO / CBO / Head of External Innovation hire (last 90 days)** — new exec buys tools and partnerships in their first 100 days to put their stamp on pipeline. Source: LinkedIn announcements, company press release, FierceBiotech.
3. **Pipeline-gap signal: discontinuation or Phase 2 failure** — creates explicit hole the BD team is now tasked to fill. Source: 8-K filings, JPMorgan Healthcare Conference talks, earnings transcripts.
4. **Public statement on external-innovation strategy** — JLABS Quick Fire Challenge, Lilly Catalyze360 expansion, Pfizer CBIO press tour, Sabry partnering letter. Source: company-published, traceable.
5. **Hiring "Search & Evaluation," "External Innovation," or "Director, BD Licensing" in last 60 days** — *the strongest active-buying signal*. If they're recruiting the buyer, they're about to buy. Source: LinkedIn Jobs, Greenhouse, Lever, company careers pages.

Secondary-tier triggers we accepted: Big Pharma 10-K "Risk Factors" sections that explicitly cite reliance on external partnerships; BIO Partnering attendee lists; recent academic spinout deal signaling open partnering door.

## 5. Suggested first-line variants per persona

> The CSV's **Why Now** column is the opener. The variants below are the next-line prompt — use sparingly, keep the email under 80 words.

**Emerging-biotech CEO (post-Series A/B):**
> "Saw the [round] close — congrats. EdenRadar pulls licensable assets directly from [N] US TTOs in your therapeutic area, ranked by readiness and IP status. Worth 15 minutes to see whether anything maps to the platform you're scaling?"

**Mid-cap CSO (LOE pressure / pipeline expansion):**
> "Noticed [program] readouts pushed BD into the next-12-months conversation on the Q[N] call. We index [therapy area] assets across the US tech-transfer corridor — could pull a short-list relevant to [target/MOA] before [JPM / BIO] if useful."

**Big Pharma External Innovation lead:**
> "We index every TTO listing across the US weekly — [N] universities, [N] active assets in [TA] this quarter, all normalized with patent + readiness signals. Built specifically so search-and-evaluation teams stop browsing 30 portals one at a time. 15 min next week?"

**Big Pharma CSO / Head of R&D (deeper pitch):**
> "[Trigger]. EdenRadar is built for the [J&J Innovation / Catalyze360 / Pharma Partnering] motion — surfaces academic IP at the right readiness and IP-status point, with the inventor-contact path baked in. Happy to send a sample [TA] dossier."

**VC / Venture studio Managing Director:**
> "We monitor every US TTO listing daily — primarily for in-house BD teams, but a handful of venture studios use us to scout NewCo opportunities at the IP-disclosure stage. Worth a look at how [studio name] would source through it?"

**VC / Studio EIR / Operating Partner (for NewCo formation):**
> "Most of your peers' NewCos start from a single PI relationship. We index every academic disclosure in the US weekly — [N] new biotech disclosures last week alone, with patent-status + inventor-contact resolved. Useful for your next NewCo?"

## 6. Disqualification rules used

We dropped a row when **any** of these were true:

- **Non-US HQ** (per task scope, strictly enforced). EU / UK / Canadian / Australian / Asian parents excluded even when they have major US R&D presence — i.e., **Sanofi, Novartis, Takeda, AstraZeneca, GSK, Bayer, Roche, Boehringer, Daiichi Sankyo, Otsuka, Astellas, EMD Serono are all excluded**. The single permitted exception is **Genentech** because, as a Roche subsidiary headquartered in South San Francisco with its own legal entity, US R&D leadership, and Pharma Partnering function, it operates as the US dealmaker for Roche's external innovation; partnering inquiries are received and decided in San Francisco. Sanofi US (Cambridge, MA) is excluded. Takeda US (Cambridge, MA) is excluded. Novartis Biomedical Research US (Cambridge, MA) is excluded.
- **<30 people** for emerging biotech (no BD function, founder is the buyer — too early).
- **Already closed a >$200M platform deal in last 6 months** that satisfies the same pipeline gap (e.g., a TYK2-focused biotech that just licensed a TYK2 program).
- **Pure CDMO / services / biosimilar manufacturer** (not an asset buyer).
- **Recent layoffs of >25% R&D headcount** (no BD budget; in retreat).
- **Already represented under a stronger segment** (e.g., a biotech that's both a VC portfolio co and an emerging biotech is kept on the Emerging row, not duplicated under VC).
- **Asset acquired by Big Pharma since trigger date** (e.g., Karuna, Mirati, Carmot, Chinook excluded — pipelines now live inside acquirer rows).

| **Tier 1** | ICP fit + active trigger in last 30 days, OR known active-buying program (JLABS / Catalyze360 / Foresite Labs / Raven) | Personalized 1:1 email + LinkedIn touch + offer of dossier sample. SDR owns. |

| **Tier 3** | Partial fit (e.g., narrow TA, smaller team, older trigger) | Newsletter / nurture; revisit in 90 days. |

Distribution across this list: roughly **35% Tier 1 / 50% Tier 2 / 15% Tier 3**.

## 8. Email column — what it actually is

Every email in the CSV is suffixed `[pattern]` because **none of these were verified through a deliverability tool**. They are inferred from the company's documented public email format (e.g., `first.last@company.com` for Pfizer/Merck/Lilly, `flast@bms.com` for BMS, `firstinitial+lastname@gilead.com`, etc.). The user's deliverability tool of choice (NeverBounce, ZeroBounce, Mailtester) should run the full column before send. Expect 60–80% verified-deliverable rate as typical for cold B2B.

For LinkedIn URLs: **101 of 105 are verified canonical profile URLs** (`linkedin.com/in/<slug>`) — sourced via web search and strictly name-matched against the result slug or page title before acceptance. The remaining **4 fall back to people-search URLs** (suffix ` [search]`) because no public canonical profile could be confirmed; click-through resolves to the correct person via name + company keywords. Spot-check before send — biotech BD turnover runs ~15–20%/year, so handles can lapse.

## 9. Refresh cadence

- **30-day refresh**: Tier 1 triggers (funding rounds, hires) decay fastest. Re-run the trigger column for Tier 1 rows monthly.
- **90-day refresh**: Full list re-verification. Companies move, people leave, BD priorities shift. Plan to re-source the entire list every 90 days.
- **12-month refresh**: ICP definitions themselves should be revisited annually. As EdenRadar surfaces specific therapeutic-area assets, the ICP can sharpen — e.g., if 60% of TTO inventory is oncology, weight emerging-biotech rows toward oncology platforms.

## 10. Methodology footnotes

- **No code changes** to the EdenRadar product were made for this deliverable. This is pure research output.
- **Triggers are public-source-traceable**. Every "Why Now" maps to a publicly disclosed event (round close, hire announcement, earnings-call language, conference talk, BLA/NDA filing). Where exact day-of-month was unclear, dates were rounded to the start of the publicly disclosed week or month.
- **Contact names are best-effort using publicly disclosed leadership** (proxy filings, SEC filings, company "leadership" pages, press releases, LinkedIn). For any single name, cross-check against the company's current "Leadership" page before send — biotech BD turnover runs ~15–20% per year.
- **Segment-coverage skew**: the spec asked for ~40/30/15/15. Final list lands at 44/31/15/15 (105 total) because a handful of high-confidence emerging + mid-cap rows surfaced during sourcing that were too valuable to drop.
- **Excluded sources used in process**: Apollo, ZoomInfo, Sales Navigator (no direct access). Rows here are the kind of high-quality enrichment a Clay/Apollo/ZoomInfo waterfall would produce — but the user should still run the list through their data tool of choice before sending, both for email-deliverability and for any updated direct-dial numbers.
