Build the next version of HelixRadar as a brokerage-grade biotech asset matchmaking platform.

HelixRadar is not just a scientific search engine. It is a platform that helps identify under-the-radar, potentially licensable biotech assets and match them to the interests of large pharma, mid-sized pharma, small pharma, venture investors, and biotech founders.

The core business goal is to act as a matchmaker between research-originated assets and commercial buyers.

This product should ingest signals from multiple scientific, clinical, IP, and university innovation sources, normalize those signals into structured asset records, score each asset for matchmaking value, and generate buyer-facing opportunity briefs and reports.

The current app already exists. Expand it substantially.

Do not rebuild from scratch. Extend the existing HelixRadar app.

Do not add commentary. Implement the product.

⸻

Product Goal

HelixRadar should answer:
	•	What are the most promising under-the-radar biotech assets?
	•	Which of them appear early, novel, and commercially relevant?
	•	Which appear licensable or likely reachable?
	•	Which fit a specific buyer thesis?

The app should prioritize brokerage value, not raw search volume.

The system should treat:
	•	papers
	•	preprints
	•	trials
	•	patents
	•	university licensing listings

as evidence signals

and treat the asset as the core object.

⸻

Core Product Model

HelixRadar should be structured around four layers:

1. Signal Ingestion

Pull records from multiple relevant external sources.

2. Asset Normalization

Convert heterogeneous records into a common asset schema.

3. Matchmaking Scoring

Score each asset based on novelty, freshness, fit, licensability, readiness, and competition.

4. Buyer-Facing Outputs

Produce:
	•	ranked search results
	•	asset dossiers
	•	buyer-specific match reports

Pipeline Modules

Create a clean pipeline under lib/pipeline/.

Required modules:
	•	collectSignals.ts
	•	normalizeSignals.ts
	•	clusterAssets.ts
	•	scoreAssets.ts
	•	generateReport.ts
	•	generateDossier.ts

collectSignals.ts

Calls all source modules in parallel and returns RawSignal[].

normalizeSignals.ts

Uses deterministic parsing plus LLM extraction to convert raw text into structured candidate asset attributes.

Extract:
	•	asset_name
	•	target
	•	modality
	•	indication
	•	development_stage
	•	owner / institution
	•	licensing hints
	•	patent hints

clusterAssets.ts

Merge related signals into candidate assets.

Use heuristics such as:
	•	similar asset_name
	•	similar target
	•	same owner/institution
	•	overlapping indication
	•	shared drug/intervention names

This does not have to be perfect. It just needs to produce useful grouped candidate assets.

scoreAssets.ts

Apply matchmaking scoring.

generateReport.ts

Use the ranked assets plus buyer profile to generate a buyer-facing report.

generateDossier.ts

Use one asset plus its supporting signals to generate a detailed commercial opportunity brief.

Scoring Model

Implement a first-pass deterministic scoring engine.

Each asset should receive:

Novelty Score

Higher when:
	•	signal is recent
	•	limited source count but strong evidence
	•	preprint or university-origin signals appear early
	•	less evidence of broad commercial saturation

Freshness Score

Higher when:
	•	recent publication / posting / filing date
	•	recent signal activity

Readiness Score

Higher when:
	•	strong preclinical evidence
	•	trial-stage confirmation
	•	patent presence
	•	multiple corroborating signals

Licensability Score

Higher when:
	•	university or academic ownership
	•	explicit licensing listing
	•	no obvious large pharma ownership
	•	contactable innovation office
	•	tech transfer source present

Fit Score

Higher when:
	•	target, modality, indication, and stage align closely with the buyer profile

Competition Score

This should penalize assets that appear overly crowded or already dominated by obvious major players.
Use whatever proxies are feasible now:
	•	many highly similar recent signals
	•	obvious large commercial sponsors
	•	later-stage crowded assets

Total Score

Weighted sum of the above.

Return a full score breakdown for transparency.
