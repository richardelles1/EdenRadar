What I'd push back on:

  1. Everything is the same color and the same visual weight
  Every card, every section header, every bar uses the same green primary + muted-foreground palette.
  There's no hierarchy — the stats grid looks identical to the recent searches list, which looks
  identical to the institutions grid. The only visual break is the orange Flame icon on Converging
  Areas. The email template has stage-colored badges (blue Phase 2, purple Phase 1, amber preclinical)
   — that logic exists but isn't used anywhere on the dashboard. Even just coloring the stat card
  borders or using those stage colors on the ticker would break the visual monotony.

  2. The four stat cards describe the corpus, not the user
  "Assets Indexed: 47,000", "Top Modality: Small Molecule" — these are the same for every user. They
  tell a new buyer nothing about their own situation. More useful at a glance: active alerts, pipeline
   items, new assets matching their focus since last login, or days since last enrichment run. The
  corpus stats belong on the Admin page or a "Market Overview" panel, not as the hero stats.

  3. The rotating ticker is undersized for what it represents
  NewlyIndexedCard and NewAlertsCard are in a 2-col grid at the bottom, min-h-[200px], and show one
  asset at a time with a 1×1px dot indicator. These are actually the highest-signal content on the
  page — what just came in — but they're given less visual presence than the modality bar chart. The
  ticker concept is right but the card should probably be taller, the asset name larger, and the
  institution treatment more prominent.

  4. The subtitle "Your TTO asset intelligence dashboard" is dead weight
  It's below the greeting on every single load. Users know where they are. Replace it with something
  live: "4 new assets indexed since your last visit" or "2 alerts active · last match 3 hours ago" —
  something that makes the page feel like a real-time pulse rather than a static header.

  5. The 10px text is borderline
  text-[10px] appears in therapy area pills, convergence signal metadata, ticker dot labels,
  institution card counts. That's rendering at ~10px, which at normal display density is right at the
  edge of legibility and will look thin on non-retina screens. Bumping these to text-xs (12px)
  consistently would feel more substantial without breaking the layout.

  Overall: the page looks like a well-built internal tool, but not yet like a premium intelligence
  product. The fix isn't a redesign — it's: personalize the hero stats, give the ticker cards more
  presence, and introduce one or two more accent colors so the eye has somewhere to land. The bones
  are there.
