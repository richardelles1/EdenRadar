You’re already very close to the right shape of product, and your instinct about Ovid Synthesis is exactly right. Ovid didn’t win because it had the most features — it won because it turned research into a structured workflow and made the output credible.

So the real question is not “what features are missing?” but:

What makes researchers trust and rely on the platform?

From the way you described the system, you already have:
	•	structured project template
	•	collapsible sections
	•	literature attachments
	•	research brief output
	•	multi-source library
	•	discovery cards → industry
	•	collaboration (in progress)

That’s already a strong core.

To become best-in-class, the additions should do one of four things:
	1.	increase trust
	2.	reduce thinking friction
	3.	generate credible outputs
	4.	create network effects

Below are the additions that move the needle most.

⸻

1. Evidence Grading (Huge Trust Feature)

Systematic research tools always include evidence grading.

Every attached paper could get a quick rating.

Example fields:

Evidence strength
Study type
Sample size
Replication status

Dropdown example:

Meta-analysis
Randomized clinical trial
Cohort study
Preclinical study
Computational study

Then a confidence rating:

High confidence
Moderate
Low

Why this matters:

Researchers instantly understand the quality of the literature base.

This is one of the quiet things Ovid does very well.

⸻

2. Contradiction Detection

Elite research tools show conflicting findings.

When multiple papers are attached:

Add a small section:

Conflicting Evidence

Researchers summarize:

Paper A shows positive correlation
Paper B shows no effect
Paper C shows inverse relationship

This is extremely valuable for systematic thinking.

⸻

3. Structured Research Timeline

Projects benefit from a simple timeline.

Concept
Literature review
Experimental design
Pilot data
Validation
Publication
Commercialization

Researchers can update progress.

Industry can later see where the discovery sits.

⸻

4. Research Map / Concept Graph

A visual element can be extremely powerful.

Imagine a small graph showing:

Project
│
├── Papers
├── Patents
├── Grants
├── Datasets
└── Collaborators

It becomes a knowledge map for the project.

This doesn’t need to be fancy.

Even a simple relationship graph is powerful.

⸻

5. Exportable Research Briefs

Your brief generation idea is excellent.

Add export formats:

PDF
Grant brief
Industry brief
Lab meeting summary

Each output adjusts tone slightly.

Example:

Scientific summary
Commercialization summary
Executive summary

This makes the platform useful beyond the portal itself.

⸻

6. Research Signal Alerts

You already have alerts, but you can make them smarter.

Example:

New paper related to this project
New grant in your research area
New patent filed
New clinical trial

This keeps projects alive over time.

⸻

7. Discovery Readiness Score

This will be extremely valuable later.

Automatically estimate:

Discovery Readiness Score

Based on:

literature strength
grant funding
patent status
experimental stage

Example output:

Readiness Score: 72/100

Industry users love this.

⸻

8. Project Templates

Instead of one template, allow:

Experimental study
Clinical research
Computational research
Review study
Commercialization concept

This slightly changes the fields.

It reduces friction.

⸻

9. Lab / Team Mode

If you eventually allow labs to join:

Lab workspace
Shared projects
Shared discovery pipeline

This is how the tool spreads across universities.

⸻

10. Research Provenance Log

This is subtle but powerful.

Track:

who added paper
who edited hypothesis
who updated method

Researchers appreciate transparent history.

⸻

The One Feature That Could Make Your Platform Unique

Your industry bridge is the differentiator.

You should lean into it.

Add a section inside each project:

Industry Relevance

Fields like:

market applications
competitive technologies
commercial timeline

This is what transforms:

research → opportunity

No academic tool really does this well.

What You Should NOT Do

Avoid turning the platform into:

another literature search engine

You already solved that by aggregating sources.

The product should focus on:

structured research thinking
+
discovery creation
+
commercialization pipeline
