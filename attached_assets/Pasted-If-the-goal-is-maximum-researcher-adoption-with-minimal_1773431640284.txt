If the goal is maximum researcher adoption with minimal complexity, the three highest-impact additions are the ones that:
	•	reduce work researchers already do manually
	•	produce credible outputs they can reuse
	•	integrate directly into their workflow

Everything else is nice, but these three create real stickiness.

⸻

1. Evidence Extraction Tables

This is arguably the single most useful feature for researchers doing structured analysis.

Researchers currently read papers and manually summarize them in spreadsheets or notes. If your platform lets them extract structured data directly from each paper, it becomes much more valuable.

Example table fields:
	•	Study title
	•	Study type
	•	Sample size
	•	Population
	•	Intervention
	•	Outcome
	•	Key findings
	•	Evidence strength

Once multiple studies are added, the platform automatically generates a comparison table across studies.

This is extremely valuable for:
	•	systematic reviews
	•	grant proposals
	•	literature reviews
	•	research briefs

It turns your tool from a paper organizer into an analysis tool.

⸻

2. Exportable Research Outputs

Researchers adopt tools that generate usable documents.

If your project can export structured outputs, the platform becomes part of their writing workflow.

Examples of outputs:
	•	Research brief (PDF)
	•	Grant proposal summary
	•	Literature review summary
	•	Industry opportunity brief

Even a simple export like:

Executive Summary
Research Question
Evidence Summary
Methods
Key Findings
Commercialization Potential

is extremely useful.

Researchers often need to generate documents like this for:
	•	lab meetings
	•	grant proposals
	•	investor decks
	•	collaborators

If your tool can generate those automatically, it saves time.

⸻

3. Project-Linked Research Alerts

Once a researcher creates a project, the platform should continuously monitor relevant signals.

Example alerts:
	•	new papers related to the project topic
	•	new patents filed
	•	new grants announced
	•	new clinical trials started

This turns the platform into a live intelligence system instead of a static workspace.

Researchers receive notifications like:

“3 new publications related to your project topic were published this week.”

This creates ongoing engagement and keeps projects updated.

⸻

Why These Three Matter Most

Together they form a powerful loop:
	1.	Evidence extraction tables
→ organize research evidence.
	2.	Exportable outputs
→ turn projects into credible documents.
	3.	Research alerts
→ keep projects continuously updated.

This combination makes the platform useful for daily work, not just occasional research.

⸻

Bonus (Very Close #4)

Your Discovery Card → Industry pipeline is already a strong differentiator.

If the system can automatically convert a completed research project into an industry-ready summary, that becomes a natural next step in the workflow.

⸻

In short, the platform becomes:

research workspace
→ evidence analysis
→ research brief
→ industry opportunity

That’s a workflow most research tools don’t offer.
