New biotech assets entered the TTO licensing pipeline in Q2 2026
Quarter-on-quarter intake reached its strongest level since 2023, up 14% from the same period last year. The majority of these assets have not yet appeared in any active licensing conversation — not because of access restrictions, but because BD search infrastructure still defaults to the same visible tier of institutions it always has.
02 · What's Moving
Signal 01
Oncology competition is narrowing around a shrinking visible set. BD teams are contesting the same well-indexed assets from the same handful of institutions. A secondary tier of oncology filings from less-visible universities remains almost entirely uncontested. Concentration of attention is the constraint, not supply.
Signal 02
CNS and rare metabolic filings are outpacing licensing activity by a widening margin. Lower deal competition and a growing cluster of pre-clinical assets with early efficacy signals align well with mid-size pharma's current search criteria. These categories are materially underdiscovered relative to their filing volume.
Signal 03
Gene silencing mechanisms are entering the pipeline at an accelerated rate. Q2 saw a clustering of siRNA and antisense-based assets filed within a narrow window, spanning CNS, oncology, and rare disease indications. The licensing window for early-stage assets in this mechanism class tends to close quickly once clinical validation starts arriving from other programmes.

03 · Therapeutic Spotlight
This quarter's focus area
Central Nervous System

CNS represents one of the most structurally underdiscovered categories in the academic licensing market. Filing volume has grown steadily since 2022, but the share of CNS assets that reach active licensing conversations remains disproportionately low — a gap driven by discoverability, not demand.

The assets most likely to go unnoticed are early pre-clinical programs at regional and mid-tier research universities, where TTO outreach capacity is limited. Several have produced assets with validated mechanisms that have not yet attracted a qualified inquiry.

04 · The Brief Take
"The licensing market conflates recency with relevance. A three-year-old pre-clinical asset with clean IP and a strong institution behind it is frequently more valuable than a newly filed one with unclear protection. The industry's search infrastructure doesn't reflect that — it sorts by date, not by fit."
— EdenNX Editorial · June 2026
